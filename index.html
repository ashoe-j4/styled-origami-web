INTERESTING FACTS ABOUT CAMELS

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  • Camels sleep standing up to keep themselves safe from predators.

INTERESTING FACTS ABOUT CHAMELEONS

  • Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads.
  • Chameleons don't have ears that can detect anything other than a low vibration.
  • Chameleons can swim very well.

INTERESTING FACTS ABOUT PIGEONS

  • Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.

INTERSTING FACTS ABOUT TEDDY BEARS

  • The teddy bear was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
  • Teddy bears and other toys account for twenty two deaths each year, and nearly one hundred and fifty thousand injuries.
  • The Teddy Bears' Picnic song was originally called The Teddy Bear Two Step.

INTERSTING FACTS ABOUT PANDAS

  • An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day.
  • Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
  • A panda's paw has six fingers.

INTERESTING FACTS ABOUT CICADAS

  • Cicadas have 3 eyes.
  • Cicadas emerge every 17 years.
  • Male cicadas make a high-pitched mating sound to attract females.
